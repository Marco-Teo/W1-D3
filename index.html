Schiacciata toscana

Wow your taste buds with this festive focaccia appetizer topped with sweet caramelized onions, tart cranberries, and creamy brie. It will become your new favorite holiday starter!

Ingredients

For the schiacciata

  • All-purpose flour: 200g
  • Manitoba flour: 300g
  • Cold water: 340g
  • Fresh brewer’s yeast: 3g
  • Fine salt: 5g
  • Honey: 5g
  • Extra virgin olive oil: 20g

For the topping:

  • Extra virgin olive oil: as needed (q.b.)
  • Fine salt: as needed (q.b.)
  • 1-2 tablespoons olive oil, for brushing on top

Method

  1. Make the schiacciata

    Perfect as an appetizer and to share with friends. What is it? It’s schiacciata! Shall we start? In a bowl, I combine the flours: Manitoba flour and all-purpose flour. I dust my hands with flour, take the yeast, and crumble it in like this. I start pouring in cold water, leaving some aside, and begin kneading. I add the remaining water and keep kneading. Then, I add some olive oil and honey, and now I knead by hand. Finally, I add the salt. Remember, always add the salt at the end so it doesn’t come into direct contact with the yeast. I transfer the dough into a greased bowl. The greased bowl will help the dough rise more easily. Why is that? Because the dough will slide along the edges. Now I cover the dough with plastic wrap and place it in the oven with the light on for about an hour to an hour and a half, until it doubles in size. First, I wet the countertop and my hands. This helps prevent the dough from sticking to either the counter or my hands. Now, I perform the folds three times to strengthen the dough. After the final round of folds, let the dough rest one last time for another 30 minutes. Spread the dough on a baking tray. Make the classic dimples. Now, add a bit more water and oil. Remember, in Tuscany, schiacciata is nicely oily and also quite salty. Bake it at 230°C (445°F) for 7 minutes. Then, lower the oven temperature to 190°C (375°F) and bake for 9 minutes, always in static mode. Finally, the last step—the sweet finish—remove the schiacciata, place it on a cooling rack, and return it to the oven at 190°C (375°F) for another 9 minutes.
